For five years, I poured my energy into learning, building, and growing. Along the way, I discovered not just how to win, but how important it is to enjoy the journey and stay connected to the world around me.


### **Five Years of Focus and Growth**



In 2020, a setback inspired me to work harder and dream bigger. I wanted to stand out and make a difference. So, I dove into coding, game development, and creative projects—sometimes losing track of time in the best way.

![The Start](/images/posts/37a.jpg)

For two years, I explored the world of game engines and programming. Coding became my playground and my passion. I spent months experimenting with shaders and simulations, learning new things every day.

In 2022, I started college. My focus was on building projects and challenging myself. I even created my own programming language in C++. It was exciting to see ideas come to life and to keep pushing my limits.

![The Machine](/images/posts/37b.jpg)


December 2023 brought my first big win at a national hackathon. That moment sparked a series of achievements—sometimes winning every weekend, sometimes just learning something new. The pace was fast, and the challenges were real, but so was the excitement.

Of course, there were tough moments. Teams changed, projects shifted, and sometimes things didn’t go as planned. But every challenge was a chance to adapt, grow, and keep moving forward.

![Competition](/images/posts/37c.jpg)



I wrote books, published software, and kept learning. In the summer of 2025, I interned at a fintech startup and built real-time monitoring systems. It was rewarding to see my work make a difference.

![Wins](/images/posts/37d.jpg)

Placement season brought new opportunities and decisions. Sometimes things worked out, sometimes they didn’t—but every experience taught me something valuable about myself and the world.

By the end of 2025, I was leading an AI project at a startup accelerator. It felt great to see how far I’d come.

![Placement](/images/posts/37e.jpg)



---

### **Looking Forward: Balance and Connection**

2026 is about finding balance—enjoying both the work and the moments in between. I want to keep building, but also reconnect with friends, family, and the world outside my screen.

![The Cost](/images/posts/37f.jpg)

For years, I focused on solving problems and reaching goals. Now, I’m learning to set healthy boundaries, say yes to new experiences, and appreciate the simple joys of life.

I’m proud of what I’ve achieved, but even more excited for what’s next. The journey isn’t just about winning; it’s about growing, connecting, and enjoying every step along the way.
